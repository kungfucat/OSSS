Our Rules and Regulations

General Rules of the School


  • Every student should regularly carry with him/her the school diary. If a student loses the diary, he/she will be subject to disciplinary action. A fine of â‚¹ 125/- will be levied and a new diary will have to be purchased with necessary remarks about the loss in it.

  • Every student must wear school uniform on all school days and on all school functions. All children are expected to come to school daily in freshly laundered uniform with clean socks and well-polished shoes. Any child who comes to school in a slovenly condition will be sent home.

  • Students should take care of school property. They should not write on or scratch the desks, chairs, walls or doors of the school nor should they damage property belonging to others. Any damage done to the school property must be remitted by the offender. Any one who notices something damaged should report the matter even if he or she does not know who has done it.

  • A student who uses unfair means during tests will be given a zero in the subject and a warning letter. Repetition of the same will result in dismissal. Any student who is found tampering with marks in the Report Card and/or other school certificates will be asked to leave the school. These students will not be eligible for any award
  • Students are not allowed to use the school telephone without permission of the Principal nor will they be allowed to answer telephone calls during class hours. They will not be allowed to receive letters or other mail without the permission of the Principal

  • Students are forbidden to bring crackers, explosives or any other dangerous material to school. Bursting crackers or throwing colours on one another/or painting faces within the school premises will lead to expulsion of the student from the school.



The student can be removed by school authorities on any one of the following grounds at any given time :


  • Constant weakness in studies.

  • Irregularity in attendance.

  • Behaviour problem.

  • Indulging in fights with others.

  • Non-payment of fee or chronic irregularity in payment.

  • Moral breach considered serious by school authorities.

  • Bringing disrepute to the school in any manner whatsoever.

  • Being a ward of ill-mannered, discourteous & disrespectful parents towards the teaching staff.


RULES CONCERNING ABSENCE


  • Leave of absence is not granted except on Prior Written Application from Parent/Guardian except in case of unforeseen circumstances.
  • For leave of absence for more than 6 days, parents must meet the Principal and get the leave granted.
  • Repeated absence without leave or unexplained absence for more than five working days, renders the student liable to have his/her name struck off the rolls. Re-admission will have to be sought.
  • Any student having less than 75% of the attendance shall not be allowed to appear in the final examination. Medical leave/other leave will be counted as absent for counting of the attendance for the final examination.
  • The student suffering from the disease must observe the prescribed period of quarantine before returning to class.
